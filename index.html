Swami Vivekananda, born Narendranath Datta, was an Indian Hindu monk, philosopher, author, religious teacher, and the chief disciple of the Indian mystic Ramakrishna.

About the Legend

☛ Swami Vivekananda was a Hindu monk and a person of literature in India.

☛ He had born in Calcutta as Narendra Dutta during the British government on 12 January 1863.

☛ He completed graduation in 1884 and started reading the works of many renowned writers and authors.

☛ He met his spiritual Guru Swami Ramakrishna in 1881.

☛ In 1897, he founded Ramakrishna Math and Ramakrishna foundation on the name of his Guru.

☛ He had attended and delivered a speech at the Institute of Chicago on 11 September 1893 that made him globally famous.

☛ He established the branches of Ramakrishna Mission also in other countries.

☛ He had founded Shanti Ashram in California and many Vedanta Societies in San Francisco.

☛ He has also contributed to the literary field with his works Raja Yoga, My Master, Bhakti Yoga, etc.

☛ Swami Vivekananda had a strong memory since his childhood.

☛ He had read all Hindu scriptures like Vedas, Upanishads, Bhagwat Geeta, Ramayana, and Mahabharata.

☛ During his visit to the UK, he met Margaret Elizabeth Noble who later became his disciple known as Sister Nivedita.

☛ Swami Vivekananda never allowed a woman to enter in his monasteries.

☛ His speech in Chicago made him globally famous

☛ He followed his guru Ramakrishna throughout his life and took all his responsibilities after his death.

☛ People say that Swami Vivekananda suffered from 31 diseases.

☛ He had already announced that he would not achieve the age of 40 and died at the age of 39.

☛ The birth anniversary of Swami Vivekananda in India is celebrated as National Youth Day.
